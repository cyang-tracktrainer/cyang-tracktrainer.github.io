What is the Track Trainer?

The Track Trainer is a versatile rehabilitation device that transforms rehabilitation into a stimulating, customizable game. For stroke patients struggling with hand and arm motor disability who are frustrated with current rehabilitation regimens, our device is an enjoyable and affordable solution that will bring recovery well within reach.

For Patients

The Track Trainer gives stroke patients an engaging and intuitive way to improve arm and hand coordination, regardless of their level of disability. It can be used for both at-home and therapist-assisted training.

For Therapists

The modular design of the Track Trainer allows it to be customized for a wide range of patients. Furthermore, our device's affordable price point makes it accessible even to low-budget rehabilitation centers.

The Device

Functional Arm / Hand Rehabilitation

The Track Trainer simulates many real world functions for day to day living like pushing a button, turning a knob, and swiping a credit card. With its customizable components, the Track Trainer is designed to service a whole spectrum of paralysis patients to regain motion in their arms.

How It Works

1

At its core, the Track Trainer is comprised of a track through which patients guide a joystick.

2

Along the track, several gates block the motion of the joystick. To open the gates, patients must complete the indicated task on the task board.

3

Patients complete tasks to open the gates along the track. Each task mirrors necessary every-day functions. Moreover, these tasks can be customized to best suit the patient.

4

The time a patient takes to complete one lap of the track is recorded by the Track Trainer. Patients can record these times to see, first-hand, the improvement in their motor function!

Intrinsically Engaging

The Track Trainer was primarily designed to improve the rehabilitation experience of patients who struggle with noncompliance. For such patients, current rehabilitation devices lack excitement and are therefore difficult to use repeatedly for months at a time.

The Track Trainer, by contrast, combines technology and work-reward feedback systems to give patients an engaging and responsive experience. With the Track Trainer, patients are visually engaged by electronically controlled elements such as automatically activated gates and lights. Moreover, patients are inspired to remain dedicated to their therapy regimens – every patient has the opportunity to beat their personal record for lap time each time they use the Track Trainer.

Completely Customizable

One of Track Trainer’s biggest asset is that it provides a treatment possibility for many patients recovering from motor dysfunction: there are multiple ways the Track Trainer can be customized to cater to patients’ varying needs.

Joystick Variation

Patients with severe motor deficits can use our specially designed joystick that secures their hand to the joystick. This allows patients who can’t grip well to still use the device. On the other hand, our round top joystick allows high-level patients to practice gripping an object that is difficult to hold.

Task Variation

Patients can also choose to turn certain tasks on or off. If a recovering individual specifically needs to practice twisting motions, they can enable the tasks that involve twisting while disabling all other tasks. The Track Trainer will subsequently only test this motion. Similarly, if a certain task is too difficult for a patient, they can simply switch that task off.

At-Home Use

The Track Trainer is portable and intuitive, making it an ideal device for at-home use.
